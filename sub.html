The Jianyuan Ring Surrounding the Taiping Ocean
A Journey Through
Two rings around the Pacific. One is the Ring of Fire.

It is called the Pacific Rim Volcanic Belt because it is a ring of volcanoes that are still active.

The other is "Ring of Life." As a result of the movement of the earth due to the activity of the volcano and the creation of various natural environments, many living creatures have come to live there.

The connection of abundant life is called the Pacific Rim Life Zone.

Kaiyukan will tell you about the Pacific Ocean and the many natural environments and creatures that surround it.

PERMANENT EXHIBITION

Permanent Exhibition

Japan Forest

Japan Forest

The sound of water in the waterfall echoing in the forest, the greenery of the moss that increases the wetness and vividness of the droplets, and the trees that change their expression every season. And the creatures that live by the waters of the mountain stream. If otters were still living in the Japan, this scene might have spread.

Aleutian Islands

Aleutian Islands

The islands stretch for 2,000 km. This nutrient-rich sea attracts many living creatures. In this aquarium lives the diving seabird Etopilika, which has the name of a beautiful beak.

Monterey Bay

Monterey Bay

California, USA. Monterey Bay continues to be one of the world's largest canyons on the seabed. Highlights include the dynamic swim through the narrow rocks and the wild life of sea lions called harems.

Gulf of Panama

Gulf of Panama

The Gulf of Panama is located in Central America. In this aquarium we are recreating the tropical dry season. On land, red bears with a characteristic tail pattern move around actively, and in the water, fish such as harisenbon are swimming.

Ecuador Rainforest

Ecuador Rainforest

Rainforests are some of the most biodiverse areas on the planet. It expresses lush greenery and wetlands. In the water swims the giant fish Pilaruk, and on the trees hides green iguanas.

Antarctica

Antarctica

Antarctica is mostly covered with ice and is called the "Continent of Ice". In such polar regions, penguins live vibrantly.

Tasman Sea

Tasman Sea

Recreates the Tasman Sea located between Australia and New Zealand. In this aquarium, the kamyrta swim dynamically.

Great
Barrier Reef

Great Barrier Reef

The world's largest coral reef continues along the northeast coast of the Australian continent. This aquarium reproduces coral cabbage that has developed towards the open ocean. The coral crevices are a great haven for fish.
